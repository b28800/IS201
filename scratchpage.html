Women's Soccer Battle for Equal Pay

Jump to Tableau Graph
  1. Background of the Issue
    • The U.S. Women's National Soccer Team (USWNT) has consistently been more successful in international competitions compared to the U.S. Men's National Soccer Team.
    • Despite their success, USWNT players have historically been paid less than their male counterparts.
  2. Key Aspects of the Disparity
    • Prize Money: Significant differences in World Cup prize money allocated by FIFA for men's and women's tournaments.
    • Salary and Bonuses: Differences in base salaries and match bonuses between USWNT and U.S. Men's National Soccer Team.
    • Sponsorships and Endorsements: Disparity in the value and number of sponsorships and endorsements.
  3. Legal Actions and Advocacy
    • USWNT filed a lawsuit against the U.S. Soccer Federation citing gender discrimination in pay and working conditions.
    • Public campaigns and support from high-profile personalities and organizations for equal pay in women's soccer.
  4. Developments and Responses
    • Settlements and Negotiations: Progress in negotiations and settlements regarding working conditions and pay equity.
